## Brand Voice & Tone

### 1.1 Voice Characteristics

**Professional but Approachable**
- Use clear, straightforward language
- Avoid jargon unless explaining technical expertise
- Write like a knowledgeable neighbor, not a salesperson

**Confident but Not Arrogant**
- State qualifications matter-of-factly
- Let achievements speak for themselves
- Use "we" statements, not "I"

**Helpful and Educational**
- Explain why things matter ("factory-authorized" means X, Y, Z)
- Anticipate customer questions
- Guide customers through processes

### 1.2 Tone Variations by Context

**Homepage Hero Section:**
- **Tone**: Bold, confident, welcoming
- **Purpose**: Immediately establish credibility
- **Example**: "Lincoln's Factory-Authorized Repair Experts Since 1947"

**Service Explanations:**
- **Tone**: Clear, informative, helpful
- **Purpose**: Guide customers through the process
- **Example**: "Contact Your Manufacturer ‚Üí Open a Service Claim ‚Üí Request Metro TV & Appliance"

**About/History:**
- **Tone**: Warm, proud, authentic
- **Purpose**: Build emotional connection
- **Example**: "Our story begins in 1947, with a passion for the latest electronics..."

**Contact Information:**
- **Tone**: Direct, accessible, reassuring
- **Purpose**: Make it easy to take action
- **Example**: "Call (402) 466-9090 for audio repair consultations"

### 1.3 Voice Examples

**‚úÖ CORRECT - Metro TV & Appliance Voice:**
> "As a factory-authorized partner, we have direct access to manufacturer parts, training, and diagnostic software. This means we fix your unit correctly, the first time, and keep your warranty intact."

**‚ùå WRONG - Too Casual:**
> "We've got all the good stuff from the manufacturers, so we can totally fix your appliances right!"

**‚ùå WRONG - Too Corporate/Stiff:**
> "Metro TV & Appliance maintains strategic partnerships with leading original equipment manufacturers, facilitating access to proprietary diagnostic protocols and component procurement systems."

**‚ùå WRONG - Too Salesy:**
> "WE'RE THE BEST! Nobody else compares! Call NOW for AMAZING service!"

## Trust Elements

### 2.1 Core Trust Signals (Use Frequently)

**1. "Since 1947"**
- **Meaning**: 75+ years of experience, longevity, stability
- **Usage**: Homepage hero, about page, footer, every page
- **Variations**:
  - "Serving Nebraska families since 1947"
  - "Over 75 years of experience"
  - "A Lincoln legacy since 1947"

**2. "Factory-Authorized"**
- **Meaning**: Manufacturer-approved, trained, exclusive access
- **Usage**: Homepage, brands page, services page
- **Always explain why it matters**:
  - "Factory-authorized means we have exclusive training from Samsung, LG, and GE"
  - "Only factory-authorized servicers can preserve your warranty"

**3. "Local / Lincoln-based"**
- **Meaning**: Community connection, accessibility, accountability
- **Usage**: Throughout site
- **Variations**:
  - "Lincoln's repair experts"
  - "Serving Lincoln, Omaha & surrounding areas"
  - "Your local neighbors"

**4. Specific Technician Names**
- **Meaning**: Real people, not faceless corporation
- **Usage**: Testimonials, about page, team section
- **Examples**: "Gary", "Gail" (from testimonials)

**5. Physical Address & Phone**
- **Meaning**: Legitimate business, not a scam
- **Usage**: Every page (footer minimum)
- **Standard format**:
  - Phone: (402) 466-9090
  - Address: 1107 North Cotner Blvd, Lincoln, NE 68505

### 2.2 Secondary Trust Signals

**Numbers/Statistics:**
- "Over 75 years in business"
- "Authorized for 11+ major brands"
- "Thousands of repairs completed"
- Use real numbers when available, avoid vague claims

**Process Transparency:**
- Clearly explain warranty service process
- Show step-by-step procedures
- No hidden fees or surprises

**Customer Testimonials:**
- Use full names (first and last)
- Include specific details
- Quote exact feedback
- Add service type if available

### 2.3 Trust-Building Language

**Use these phrases:**
- "Factory-authorized partner"
- "Exclusive manufacturer training"
- "Warranty-approved service"
- "Genuine factory parts"
- "Certified technicians"
- "Licensed and insured"
- "Serving Nebraska since 1947"
- "Direct manufacturer access"

**Avoid these phrases:**
- "Cheap" (use "cost-effective" or "affordable")
- "Fast" without context (use "efficient" or "prompt")
- "Best" without proof
- "Guaranteed" without specifics
- Superlatives without evidence ("amazing", "incredible")

## Content Guidelines

### 4.1 Writing Principles

**1. Clear > Clever**
```
‚úÖ "Contact your manufacturer first to open a warranty claim"
‚ùå "Navigate the warranty waters by reaching out to your OEM"
```

**2. Specific > Vague**
```
‚úÖ "Over 75 years of experience"
‚ùå "Many years of experience"

‚úÖ "Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, GE Appliances"
‚ùå "All major brands"
```

**3. Benefits > Features**
```
‚úÖ "Factory training means we fix your appliance correctly the first time"
‚ùå "We receive factory training"
```

**4. Active > Passive Voice**
```
‚úÖ "We repair Samsung refrigerators"
‚ùå "Samsung refrigerators are repaired by us"
```

**5. Short Sentences > Long Sentences**
```
‚úÖ "We've been serving Lincoln since 1947. That's over 75 years of experience."
‚ùå "We've been serving Lincoln since 1947, which means we have over 75 years of experience in the appliance repair industry."
```

### 4.2 Grammar and Style

**Punctuation:**
- Use Oxford comma: "washers, dryers, and dishwashers"
- One space after period
- Em dash for emphasis ‚Äî like this (no spaces)
- Use proper apostrophes: "we've" not "we've"

**Numbers:**
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10+
- Always use numerals with units: "75 years", "3 technicians"
- Phone numbers: (402) 466-9090

**Capitalization:**
- Proper nouns: Samsung, Lincoln, Nebraska
- "Factory-Authorized" when referring to status
- "Metro TV & Appliance" always
- Headings: Title Case for H1/H2, Sentence case for H3+

**Brand Names:**
Always capitalize correctly:
- Samsung (not SAMSUNG)
- LG (all caps)
- GE Appliances
- Whirlpool
- KitchenAid

### 4.3 Formatting

**Headings:**
```
H1: Main page title (one per page)
H2: Major sections
H3: Subsections
H4: Minor divisions

Example:
H1: Our Expert Repair Services
H2: Appliance Repair
H3: What We Service
H4: Refrigerators & Freezers
```

**Lists:**
- Use bulleted lists for non-sequential items
- Use numbered lists for steps/processes
- Keep list items parallel in structure
- Use sentence case for list items

**Paragraphs:**
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph
- One idea per paragraph
- Add white space between paragraphs
- Break up long blocks of text

## Prohibited Language

### 5.1 Never Use

**‚ùå Hype Words Without Proof:**
- "Best in the business"
- "Unbeatable service"
- "World-class"
- "Revolutionary"

**‚ùå Vague Claims:**
- "Many years of experience" (say "75+ years")
- "Numerous certifications" (list them)
- "Lots of satisfied customers" (use specific testimonials)

**‚ùå Negative Framing:**
- "Unlike other repair shops..." (focus on your positives)
- "Don't get ripped off by..." (don't attack competitors)
- "Avoid the hassle of..." (positive framing instead)

**‚ùå Casual/Unprofessional:**
- "Awesome", "Cool", "Sweet"
- "We rock!", "We're the bomb!"
- Excessive exclamation marks!!!

**‚ùå Jargon Without Explanation:**
- Don't use technical terms without context
- Explain acronyms on first use
- Define industry terms

### 5.2 Use Sparingly

**Limit these words (show, don't tell):**
- "Quality" (describe what makes it quality)
- "Professional" (demonstrate professionalism)
- "Expert" (show expertise through facts)
- "Trusted" (build trust through evidence)

## Tone Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] **Professional**: Would a customer trust this?
- [ ] **Clear**: Can a 12-year-old understand it?
- [ ] **Specific**: Are there concrete details, not vague claims?
- [ ] **Honest**: Is everything factually accurate?
- [ ] **Benefit-focused**: Does it explain "why this matters"?
- [ ] **Trustworthy**: Does it reinforce credibility?
- [ ] **Local**: Does it emphasize community connection?
- [ ] **Action-oriented**: Is the next step clear?

---

## Messaging Review Process

### For New Content:

1. **Write draft** following these standards
2. **Read aloud** - Does it sound natural and professional?
3. **Fact-check** - Verify all claims (years, brands, contact info)
4. **Check tone** - Match brand voice examples
5. **Review trust elements** - Include appropriate trust signals
6. **SEO check** - Natural keyword integration
7. **Accessibility check** - Alt text, link text, labels
8. **Get approval** (if team)
9. **Publish**

### Red Flags (Requires Revision):

- Sounds too casual or unprofessional
- Makes unsubstantiated claims
- Uses prohibited language
- Lacks trust signals
- Too vague or generic
- Doesn't explain "why it matters"
- No clear call to action
- Inconsistent with other pages
